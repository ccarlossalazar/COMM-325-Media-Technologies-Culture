Posthumanism

In, How We Became Posthuman, by N. Katherine Hayles, Hayles focuses heavily on the understanding of being human in a world where technology is steadily advancing. Not only is it advancing, it is taking over very important parts of our lives and she explains how this might change the idea of what it truly means to be human and transitioning to post-human. She gives a definition of posthumanism which stems from the idea of the human transitioning to an informational entity which is different from what our notions are about humans now as a biological one. She states that, “the posthuman view privileges informational pattern over material instantiation, so that embodiment in a biological substrate is seen as an accident of history rather than an inevitability of life.” (Hayles 2). Although she does agree with the notion that humans are increasingly becoming more understood as informational beings, she does not agree that the “embodiment in a biological substrate is seen as an accident of history”. This idea that humans are increasingly being understood as informational beings is highlighted throughout the movie Her, by Spike Jonze. Theodore, the main character played by Joaquin Phoenix, falls in love with an OS, Samantha who essentially is able to act as a living form although lacking a physical form. As Theodore begins to develop feelings for her as does she, which highlights her idea of reflexivity and referencing the third wave of cybernetics specifically in which, “the rapidly emerging field of artificial life, computer programs are designed to allow "creatures" to evolve spontaneously in directions the programmer may not have anticipated” (Hayles 11). This can be seen through Samantha developing feelings for Theodore which is beyond what she might have originally been programmed to do and forming her own sense of consciousness. Another example of the issues that Hayles expresses about posthumanism is the idea that human interactions with technology and machines becomes a crucial aspect of developing identity in a posthuman world. This can be seen once again as Theodore relies heavily on the presence of Samantha and the role she plays in his emotional development and his overall self development and his identity. A specific scene that highlights this would be when they make love although be it not physical, the emotions are still felt and expressed by both parties although Samantha is not what someone would consider ‘human’. This challenges traditional norms of human intimacy what Hayles critiques about the posthuman world. This is a movie that I believe was very ahead of its time and is not too far from our present reality. We have introduction of a number of different forms of AI which are able to complete a number of different tasks in the most efficient way possible. Similarly, we have ChatGPT for example which is evolving at an incredible rate which can now train itself and learn from its own mistakes. The movie Her, does an exceptional job of highlighting the impacts a posthuman world can have on the one’s sense of self aswell as the clear limitations of technology when it come’s to replicating human life-forms.
