Genre and Spectatorship

I chose to do my analysis on the original live action Transformers movie from 2007, starring Shia LaBeouf, who plays Sam WitWicky and Megan Fox, who plays Mikaela Banes. I chose this film because I am a fan of Science Fiction movies and this was a movie I had not seen since I was a kid. It is a film that requires a lot of special effects which is something that is good as it's a Science Fiction movie, but at the same time it is an action film as well so there is a lot happening at once. The quick camera cuts and fast switching of scenes makes it hard to follow during certain parts of the film impacting the overall mise-en-scene. Upon reading Neale's work I agreed with his point about genre and genre being essentially an outdated term that can’t help and explain a full story of a film. Not only this, but how films can take on multiple genres and at the end of the day it is something that continues to evolve and instead resort to using ‘film narrative’ to describe a film. Initially, I categorized Transformers simply as a science fiction and action film. However, I decided to look up the genre of the film and found that there were an additional 3 which were also adventure, comedy, and thriller. This began to make me think of what Neale was saying about genre and how, “each new genre film tends to extend this repertoire, either by adding a new element or by transgressing one of the old one’s” (Neale 167). He emphasizes that variations within genres are essential for their growth and evolution as a whole. Interestingly, in this film I also found examples of the male gaze, in the film where Megan Fox is observing the engine bay of a car. This scene features multiple shots that highlight her physical appearance, reflecting Laura Mulvey’s theory of the male gaze, which suggests that films often objectify women to appeal to a male audience. One thing I noticed about this film is that it could also in a way fall under the romance genre as Shia Labeouf and Megan Fox end up growing closer as the movie progresses ultimately until they end up together. This ties back into what I believe to be what genre says about a film and how a simple genre is too generic to be able to describe a film. On the other hand, some could also argue that this film doesn’t fall under the genre of romance due to the limited focus on romantic elements, highlighting the complexity of genre classification in film. One scene I chose to analyze was one that was toward the beginning of the film when the main character, Sam Witwicky meets Mikaela Banes for the first time. I have included the clip and upon simply watching this clip it would be hard to argue that the film has nothing to do with romance at all. However, to Neal’s point, I believe this is sort of what he means as genre being an outdated term and how genres sort of adopt new elements as they progress. What initially appeared to me to be a romantic part in the film is actually played for comedic effect, which explains why the film is classified as a comedy rather than a romance. Throughout their interactions, we see several instances where Sam’s awkwardness in flirting provides humor and adds that comedic element to the film.
